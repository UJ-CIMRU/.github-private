# About

The Computational and Industrial Research Mathematics Research Unit (CIMRU) is a research unit within the Department of Mathematics and Applied Mathematics at the University of Johannesburg.

# Vision

To be a leading pan-African and international research hub in computational and research mathematics, advancing theory, algorithms and applications that address complex scientific, engineering, industrial and societal challenges and problems.

# Mission

The mission of CIMRU is to:

* Conduct high-impact, nationally and internationally recognised research in computational and industrial mathematics.
* Develop innovative mathematical and computational methods with real-world applications and industrial relevance.
* Train postgraduate students and early-career researchers in advanced applied, computational and industrial mathematics.
* Foster interdisciplinary collaboration within academia and industry.
* Contribute to technological and socio-economic development in South Africa and beyond.

# Core Research Themes

## Numerical Analysis and Scientific Computing
* Numerical methods for ordinary and partial differential equations (e.g., Runge-Kutta methods)
* Geometric numerical integration
* Structure-preserving algorithms
* High-performance and parallel computing

## Applied and Industrial Mathematics
* Mathematical and computational modelling and simulation in engineering and physics.
* Optimisation and operations research
* Uncertainty quantification and sensitivity analysis

## Dynamical Systems and Geometry
* Symplectic geometry and geometric mechanics
* Stability theory and nonlinear dynamics
* Numerical relativity and geometric flows
* Graph dynamical systems

## Scientific Machine Learning
* Reservoir Computing (RC)
* Graph Echo State Networks (GraphESN)
* Neural ordinary differential equations (NODEs)
